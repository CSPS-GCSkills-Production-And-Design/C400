Recovery Efforts: Challenges of the Disaster Financial Assistance Arrangements (DFAA)

The federal government faces growing financial challenges due to its obligations under the Disaster Financial Assistance Agreements program.

Since 1970, the Government of Canada has paid out over $5 billion in post-disaster financial assistance through the DFAA. More than half (52%) was paid out between 2013 and 2018.

A number of factors will contribute to the continued rise of program costs in the future:

  • extreme weather conditions due to changing climate
  • demographic growth
  • the state and location of private dwellings and public infrastructure
  • the market availability and penetration of insurance against various disasters
  • the parameters of disaster financial assistance programs, including the DFAA itself

Did you know?

The DFAA Terms and Conditions were extended to March 31, 2022. This extension provides a window for Public Safety to complete an ongoing review of the DFAA with a view to ensure that it remains an effective and a sustainable instrument given the rise in frequency, impact and costs of natural disasters.
