Disaster Financial Assistance Arrangements

In the event of a large-scale natural disaster, the federal government provides assistance to the provincial and territorial governments through Disaster Financial Assistance Arrangements (DFAA).

When response and recovery costs exceed what individual provinces or territories could reasonably be expected to bear on their own, the DFAA provides the Government of Canada with a fair and equitable means of assisting the provincial and territorial governments.

Through the DFAA, assistance is paid directly to the provinces or territories, not to the individuals or communities.

Since the inception of the DFAA in 1970, the Government of Canada has provided more than $5 billion in disaster financial assistance to the provinces and territories.

Did You Know?

Following the Eastern Canada ice storm of 1998, the Government of Canada provided a total federal contribution of $525 million to Quebec, $136.4 million to Ontario, and $3.9 million to New Brunswick through the DFAA.

More recent examples of payments include $23.3 million for the 2019 Spring Flood in New Brunswick and $175 million for wildfires in British Columbia in 2017.
