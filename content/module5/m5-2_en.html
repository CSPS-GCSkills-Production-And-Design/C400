Large-scale Natural Disasters

Flooding is Canada’s costliest and most frequent natural disaster, and it can have devastating impacts on people’s homes, businesses and daily lives.

In 2019, spring flooding in Canada significantly impacted Ontario, Quebec, and New Brunswick.

  • The flood season was record breaking in Ontario. The Muskoka region, the National Capital Region, Lake Nipissing, and many norther First Nations communities along the Hudson Bay were impacted.  
  • In Quebec, 250 municipalities were affected, with towns bordering the Ottawa River and Saint Lawrence River most severely impacted. In the areas of Pointe-Calumet, Rigaud and Saint-Marthe-sur-le-Lac, one third of the city was flooded due to levee rupture.
  • In New Brunswick, Saint John, Fredericton, and Oromocto were most affected. Damage from localized flooding resulted in the closure of the Trans-Canada Highway and the registration of over 1,200 victims by the Canadian Red Cross.

As you will see, supporting communities as they pull together to recover from natural disasters is a priority for the Government of Canada (GoC).
