Strategies

As part of the recovery management process, it is important to develop and implement a recovery strategy to support short-term and long-term priorities for recovery functions, services, resources, facilities and infrastructure.

These strategies should be based on results of the hazard identification and risk assessments, business impact analysis, program constraints, operational experience and cost-benefit analysis.

Recovery strategies include:

  • interim and long-term actions to help ensure recovery and continuity of capability of the department to respond to the consequences of the hazards; and
  • measures to reduce vulnerability during the recovery period. These measures should be linked to the mitigation strategies.

The strategy should provide the basis upon which the recovery plans are based.
