EM in Action: Flooding and Wildfires

In the spring of 2019, Ontario, Quebec, and New Brunswick experienced flooding that significantly affected each province. In response, the Government of Canada provided assistance to each affected province.

  • The Government of Canada accepted a formal request for federal assistance by the province of Ontario for the deployment of 1,200 Canadian Armed Forces members in support of relief efforts.
  • Quebec reported $428 million in uninsured losses to the Government of Canada through the DFAA.
  • New Brunswick requested $23.3 million to the Government of Canada through the DFAA.

In 2016, a wildfire that spanned more than 580,000 hectares of land, engulfed parts of Northeastern Alberta and caused major damage to Fort McMurray. The entire population of Fort McMurray and surrounding areas – over 80,000 people – were evacuated.

In response, the Government of Canada provided almost $400 million through the DFAA to support impacted communities.

  • Canadians donated more than $150 million to the Canadian Red Cross.
  • The Government of Canada matched donations made by individuals within Canada for a total of $104 million, and the Government of Alberta matched donations made by individuals within the province for a total of $30 million.
  • Overall, the Canadian Red Cross raised $330 in donations in support of the Fort McMurray relief efforts.
