Recap – Lesson 3: Emergency Operation Centre

As we have seen, each level of government has a central “place” from which it manages its response activities. While these control centres go by different names, they serve the same function—to coordinate and communicate response activities to save lives and protect property and the environment.

Click on each heading for a recap of lesson three.

International

Emergency Watch and Response Centre (EWRC)

International response is coordinated by GAC and Public Safety Canada from EWRC

Government of Canada

Government Operations Centre (GOC)

Hub of Canada's strategic-level emergency response activities during an emergency, it supports Primary Departments in their response and coordinates the integrated government of Canada response. The GOC maintains contact with the provinces and territories through Public Safety Canada’s regional offices and with international partners.

Federal Government Departments

Federal Department Emergency Operations Centres

Many federal departments have important responsibilities in terms of responding to emergencies. These departments manage emergencies related to their area of responsibility from their own departmental emergency operation centres.

Provinces/Territories

Emergency Management Organizations

During an emergency, EMOs are the principal points of contact with the Government Operations Centres, through Public Safety’s regional offices.

Local Jurisdictions/Private Sector/ Non-Governmental Organizations

Emergency Operations Centres

Most municipalities, private businesses and NGOs operate EOCs as part of their emergency response systems.
