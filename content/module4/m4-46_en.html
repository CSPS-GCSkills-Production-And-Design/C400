EM in Action: Vaccination Campaign

On July 18, 2009, the World Health Organization (WHO) announced that more than 214 countries and overseas territories or communities had reported laboratory-confirmed cases of H1N1, with over 18,000 deaths globally, 428 of them in Canada.

Canada experienced two distinct waves of H1N1. The first wave occurred in the spring between April and August 2009. The second wave occurred between August 2009 and January 2010.

From the time H1N1 was discovered by laboratories in April 2009, until the time the government-approved H1N1 flu vaccines were administered in October 2009, six months had lapsed.

Fortunately, federal departments, in particular Health Canada and the Public Health Agency of Canada (PHAC) had prepared themselves for this pandemic. They were very successful in managing the largest mass immunization campaign in Canada's history and vaccinated 40% of the Canadian population.

On January 27, 2010 PHAC announced the end of the H1N1 pandemic.
