Federal Response Centre: The Government Operations Centre

The Government Operations Centre (GOC), a whole-of-government asset housed in Public Safety, is where the Government of Canada coordinates its strategic emergency response activities.

The GOC is the hub of a network of federal operations centres and provides stable, round-the-clock coordination and support to key national players in the event of national emergencies.

It operates 24 hours a day, seven days a week, compiling information from other operations centres and a wide variety of sources, both open and classified, from around the world.

Here are a few examples of its day-to-day operational responsibilities:

  • provide stable, round-the-clock monitoring, validation, reporting and response functions in relation to events of national interest
  • develop situational awareness and risk assessment products
  • coordinate the development of event-specific contingency planning
