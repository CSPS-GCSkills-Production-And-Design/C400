H1N1 in Canada

While the majority of emergencies begin locally, the H1N1 pandemic originated outside of Canada.

March 18, 2009

On March 18, 2009, Canada became aware of an outbreak of an acute respiratory illness of unknown etiology in La Gloria, Veracruz, Mexico.

April 25, 2009

On April 25, 2009, the World Health Organization (WHO) warned that the virus, identified as a member of the H1N1 family, had pandemic potential. They declared Influenza A (H1N1) the first official PHEIC (public health emergency of international concern.)

April 26, 2009

On April 26, 2009, Canada announced its first cases of H1N1: four in Nova Scotia and two in British Columbia.

April 29, 2009

On April 29, 2009, a 23-month-old Mexican child from Texas was the first confirmed H1N1 fatality in the United States. That same day, the WHO raised its pandemic alert to maximum level and the United Nations called on pharmaceutical laboratories around the world to produce vaccines to prepare for this imminent pandemic.
