Learning Objectives

This session on emergency response will enable you to:

  • Define response in the context of emergency management.
  • Identify the driving force that gives authority and responsibility to the Government of Canada to respond to an emergency (i.e., the legislation, policies, plans and frameworks).
  • Summarize the Government of Canada’s Federal Emergency Response Plan, by describing the roles of the primary, supporting and coordinating federal departments and by identifying the 13 emergency support functions.
  • Describe the Government Operation Centre, the place where the federal government coordinates its response to emergency activities and its relationship with other departmental emergency operation centres.
  • Explain the Federal Emergency Response Management System, and its six important functions that integrate an emergency response in Canada.
  • Identify the people who respond to emergencies in Canada and abroad.
