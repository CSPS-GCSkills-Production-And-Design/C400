National System: The National Emergency Response System

Other systems are utilized when emergencies threaten to overwhelm the resources of an individual province or territory and federal involvement is required. The National Emergency Response System (NERS) incorporates key principles of emergency management to coordinate the joint federal, provincial and territorial response to emergencies.

The NERS:

  • provides the links between the federal/provincial/territorial emergency response systems
  • identifies the federal/provincial/territorial interactions in the five response functions
  • facilitates federal/provincial/territorial response coordination and decision making
  • establishes standardized terminology which facilitates timely information exchange
  • describes protocol for provincial/territorial requests for federal emergency assistance
