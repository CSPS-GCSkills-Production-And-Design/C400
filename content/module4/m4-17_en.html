Recap – Lesson 2: The Plan

It’s time for a recap. Emergencies that are large and/or complex or that transcend provincial or international boundaries, such as a pandemic influenza, call for shared responsibilities. All jurisdictions, therefore, have their own emergency response plans.

Click on each heading for a recap of lesson two.

Government of Canada

Federal Emergency Response Plan (FERP)

  • all-hazards plan for a coordinated federal response in conjunction with provinces, territories, NGOs and the private sector

Federal Government Departments
  • event-specific or departmental response plans based on respective authorities
  • protocols of federal departmental response plans must align with the FERP

Provinces/Territories
  • responsible for their own emergency response plans

Local Jurisdictions/Private Sector/ Non-Governmental Organizations
  • responsible for their own emergency response plans
