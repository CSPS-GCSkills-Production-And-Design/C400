Federal Response Plan: Primary, Supporting and Coordinating Departments

It is important to understand how the government of Canada coordinates its emergency response efforts. The FERP directs specific federal departments to take different roles during emergencies. For example, with the H1N1 pandemic, Health Canada and the Public Health Agency of Canada were considered the primary departments managing all health and science issues, while the Treasury Board Secretariat was the primary department for implementing the Public Service Readiness Plan.

 

Coordinating Department

As legislated in the Emergency Management Act, Public Safety Canada is always the federal coordinating department for domestic emergencies. As such, it is responsible for engaging the relevant federal departments in the response effort.

Primary Department

A primary department is a federal department with a mandate related to a key element of an emergency. There can be more than one.

Supporting Department

A supporting department is a federal department that provides general or specialized assistance to a primary department in response to an emergency.
