International Emergency Response Centre

What about emergencies that occur outside of Canada?

When another country issues an official request for international assistance, Global Affairs Canada (GAC) coordinates the Government of Canada’s response, with the support of Public Safety Canada, from the Emergency Watch and Response Centre (EWRC).

GAC’S response to natural disasters abroad includes, but is not limited to:

  • helping Canadians in distress in the affected country through a consular affairs program
  • working with key federal departments to help ensure a timely, effective, coherent and appropriate Government of Canada response to relief and recovery requirements identified on the ground
  • communicating with the government of the affected country/countries to understand and help with their emergency response needs
  • liaising with other donor governments, international organizations and NGOs, to help ensure that Canadian assistance will not duplicate other international efforts
  • the Emergency Disaster Assistance Fund (EDAF) is a draw-down fund administered by the Canadian Red Cross Society that allows for immediate Canadian support to emergency operations in response to sudden-onset small- and medium-scale humanitarian situations, including natural disasters, non-recurrent health epidemics, and conflicts. The EDAF increases the effectiveness and timeliness of assistance to crisis-affected people
