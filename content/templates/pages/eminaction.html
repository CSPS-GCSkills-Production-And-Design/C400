EM in Action: Three Part Exercise Program

In conjunction with the Vancouver Organizing Committee for the Olympic and Paralympic Games (VANOC), municipal, provincial and federal levels of government developed a three-part exercise program. It was designed to test and validate integrated response within the Lower Mainland of British Columbia in the event of an emergency during the 2010 Winter Olympic Games.

This program was broken down into three exercises. They increased in scope, complexity and participation from exercise to exercise.

Exercise Bronze

Exercise Bronze (November 2008) involved a tabletop exercise to examine regional safety and security issues.

Exercise Silver

Exercise Silver (February 2009) incorporated the lessons learned from Exercise Bronze to practise safety and security plans, procedures and interoperability.

Exercise Gold

Exercise Gold (November 2009) included two full-scale live-action events simulating chemical, biological, radiological, nuclear and explosives (CBRNE) incidents in Richmond and Vancouver, British Columbia. These simulations provided realistic environments, using volunteers as mock casualties. This final exercise confirmed readiness for the Olympics. Over 100 agencies participated, including local health authorities, the Public Health Agency of Canada, the Vancouver Police Department, the RCMP, Vancouver Fire & Rescue, the City of Vancouver and the Canadian Forces.
