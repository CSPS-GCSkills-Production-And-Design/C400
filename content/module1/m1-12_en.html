Integrated All-Hazards Approach

In Canada, emergency management adopts an integrated, all-hazards approach to address both natural and human-induced hazards and emergencies.

What does this mean? Click on the headings below to learn more.

Integrated

Comprehensive and integrated emergency management is a shared responsibility between all levels of governments, the private sector, non-governmental organizations and individual citizens.

All Hazards

Hazards are sources of potential harm or loss. Emergencies or disasters result when a hazard interacts with a vulnerability to produce serious and adverse consequences that may, for an undetermined period of time, exceed the ability of people or their communities to cope.

Canada and its people are facing an evolving risk environment due to increasingly severe and frequent hazards and threats.

Therefore, our government must plan, prepare and be ready to respond to all potential hazards which may threaten the lives, property, environment and economy of Canada.
