Emergencies Act

In 1988, the Emergencies Act replaced the War Measures Act. It enables the federal government to provide for the safety and security of Canadians during national emergencies. The Act specifies four distinct types of national emergencies.

  • Public welfare emergencies
  • Public order emergencies
  • International emergencies
  • War emergencies

This Act is intended for special, temporary measures in extraordinary circumstances, as an instrument of last resort. It can be used only if the situation cannot be effectively dealt with under any other law of Canada.

The federal government of Canada can apply the Emergencies Act to invoke a state of emergency.
