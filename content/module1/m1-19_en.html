Emergency Management Act

The Emergency Management Act assigns specific responsibility for emergency management roles and activities to the Minister of Public Safety and ministers of other federal organizations.

Click on the headings for more information.

Minister of Public Safety
  • exercises leadership in emergency management in Canada
  • provides guidance to federal organizations on the four EM components
  • establishes policies, programs and activities for emergency management
  • coordinates EM activities among government organizations and in cooperation with the provinces and other entities
  • coordinates the Government of Canada’s response to emergencies
  • develops joint emergency management plans with the relevant United States authorities
Ministers of Federal Organizations
  • identify risks in their area of responsibility, including those related to critical infrastructure
  • prepare emergency management plans for those risks
  • maintain, test and implement those EM plans
  • conduct exercises and training for those plans
