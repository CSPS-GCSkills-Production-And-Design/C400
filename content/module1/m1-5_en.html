Changing Threats

Both natural and human-induced hazards causing emergencies are increasing in number and frequency across the world, resulting in ever-growing human suffering and economic cost.

Canada is not immune to these events. Both types of emergencies have become more prevalent in urbanized societies, and terrorist attacks on western targets are likely to persist. Moreover, the potential for various types of catastrophes is increasing due to many factors:

  • environment and climate change
  • increased urbanization
  • critical infrastructure dependencies and interdependencies
  • terrorism
  • animal and human diseases
  • increased movement of people and goods around the world

Did You Know?

  • As of 2016, 83% of the Canadian population lives in urban areas.
  • According to data compiled by the Canadian Disaster Database between 2009 and 2019, 44% of natural disasters in Canada were due to flooding, 30% due to wildfire, and 24% due to storms and severe thunderstorms.
