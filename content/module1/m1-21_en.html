Emergency Management Framework for Canada

In January 2007, the Federal/Provincial/Territorial (FPT) ministers approved an Emergency Management Framework for Canada.

The Framework supports legal and policy frameworks, programs, activities, standards and other measures to enable and inspire all emergency management partners in Canada to work in collaboration more effectively to keep Canadians safe. The Framework:

  • describes the fundamental concepts of emergency management
  • facilitates the harmonization of a national approach to emergency management
  • supports the implementation and on-going improvements of emergency management in Canada.

In 2017, the third edition of the Emergency Management Framework for Canada was announced. It now underscores the linkages between climate change and emergency management, and the need for all areas of society to work together to enhance resilience.

Did You Know?

The Emergency Management Framework is subject to revision every five years by FPT governments to ensure that it remains accurate and relevant.

It was first updated in 2011 to include sections on governance and coordination instruments, as well as an examination of future directions for federal/provincial/territorial emergency management in Canada.
