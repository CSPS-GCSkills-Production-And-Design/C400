Lesson 3: The Driving Force behind the Federal role in EM in Canada

In the emergency management field, we often refer to the “driving force”, namely the foundation supporting the federal government emergency management activities.

The driving force is made up of policies, legislative acts, and a framework that have been created, adapted and modified to provide solutions to the Government of Canada’s emergency management needs, which are ever-changing and ever-evolving.

Let’s have a closer look at the most important legislative documents and how they relate to each of the four components of EM.

  • Emergencies Act
  • Emergency Management Act
  • Federal Policy for Emergency Management
  • Emergency Management Framework for Canada

Umbrella policy piece for directing federal institutions on emergency management planning – references specific policies and plans used by PS to guide federal institutions on emergency management planning activities.
