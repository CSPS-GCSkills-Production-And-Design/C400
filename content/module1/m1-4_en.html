Types of Emergencies

There are basically two Types of Emergencies: those caused by nature, and those caused by humans, whether by error, by accident, or with intent. Take a look at these examples of natural and human-induced emergencies.

Natural Emergencies
  • animal health
  • floods
  • tornadoes
  • hurricanes
  • blizzards
  • mud/snow slides
  • storm surges
  • forest fires
  • wildland-urban interface fires
  • earthquakes
  • tsunamis
  • epidemics/human health
  • droughts
  • ice storms
  • thunderstorms
  • volcanic eruptions
Human-induced Emergencies
  • terrorism (eco, cyber, nuclear, biological, chemical)
  • radiological and nuclear
  • construction failure (bridges, buildings, water systems)
  • utility outages (electricity, natural gas)
  • transportation accidents (road, rail, air)
  • civil disturbances (protest, demonstrations)
  • large explosions, fires
  • spills of hazardous materials
  • mine accidents
  • nuclear incidents
  • cyber event
