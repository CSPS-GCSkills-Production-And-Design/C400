Federal Policy for Emergency Management

The Federal Policy for Emergency Management is an important policy that promotes an integrated and resilient whole-of-government approach to emergency management planning. This includes better prevention/mitigation of, preparedness for, response to, and recovery from emergencies.

In upcoming modules, we will review the Policy in more detail as it relates to the four components of emergency management. In the meantime, here are a few highlights:

  • announced by the Minister of Public Safety in March 2010
  • replaces the 1995 Federal Policy for Emergencies (under the former Emergency Preparedness Act)
  • directs federal organizations in their emergency management planning roles and responsibilities
