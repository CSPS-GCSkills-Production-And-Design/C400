Checkpoint- Emergency Management Roles

Let’s pause for a quick question to confirm EM roles.

Under the Emergency Management Act, the legislative foundation for EM in Canada, federal ministers have specific responsibilities for emergency management.

Question 1 of 4

1.  Who is responsible for coordinating EM activities among government organizations?

Well done. The Minister of Public Safety coordinates emergency management activities among federal organizations.

Actually, the Minister of Public Safety coordinates emergency management activities among federal organizations.


2.  Who is responsible for identifying risks in their area of responsibility?

Well done. Department heads must identify risks in their respective areas of responsibility.

Actually, department heads must identify risks in their respective areas of responsibility.


3.  Who coordinates the Government of Canada’s response to emergencies?

Well done. The Minister of Public Safety coordinates the Government of Canada’s response to emergencies.

Actually, the Minister of Public Safety coordinates the Government of Canada’s response to emergencies.


4.  Who prepares emergency management plans for the risks related to their area of responsibility?

Well done. Department heads must prepare emergency management plans to address those risks.

Actually, department heads must prepare emergency management plans to address those risks.
