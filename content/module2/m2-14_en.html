Step 2: Analyze Risks

Once identified, each risk is then assessed as to its likelihood of occurrence and the expected impact. A vulnerability assessment will help determine the impact to which a region, community, facility, or service is likely to be damaged by a particular threat, taking into consideration the level of susceptibility (vulnerability) provided by the existing protection measures.

The analysis typically takes into account a number of considerations, including:

  • demographics/population
  • economic characteristics
  • transportation routes
  • meteorology and history
  • geographical and natural features

Vulnerability exists when there is a situation or circumstance that, if left unchanged, may result in loss of life or may affect the confidentiality, integrity or availability of other mission-critical assets.
