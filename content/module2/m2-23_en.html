Structural and Non Structural Measures

Prevention and mitigation activities are actions taken to enhance the sustainability and resilience of communities by eliminating or reducing the scope and impact of emergencies in order to protect lives, property and the environment, and to avoid economic disruption.

Governments at all levels generally engage in two types of prevention/mitigation measures: structural and non-structural.

Structural Measures

  • building reinforcements to withstand natural hazards like tremors and earthquakes
  • creating flood levees
  • burying hydro lines to reduce storm damage
  • stabilizing river banks
  • installing generators
  • replacing aging water systems

Non-Structural Measures

  • regulations, policies, standards and zoning by-laws
    • building codes that meet seismic codes
  • financial assistance
  • land-use planning
  • by-law enforcement
    • control urban development, for example on flood plains or river banks
  • preservation of natural environments
  • flood plain mapping
  • communication relocation
  • improved prediction of severe weather
    • Environment and Climate Change Canada’s work on adaptation to climate change
    • Natural Resources Canada’s seismic activity work
    • collaboration among federal, provincial and American governments to activate new Atlantic tsunami early warning system
  • insurance incentives
  • public awareness, education and outreach
