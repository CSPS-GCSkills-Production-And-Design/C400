Emergency Management Act

The EMA is the legislative foundation providing for a coordinated approach to federal EM activities. The Minister of PS is responsible for exercising emergency management leadership and advising other federal ministers on their legislative responsibilities as they relate to emergency management planning. The EMA sets out clear roles and responsibilities for all federal ministers for emergencies in Canada.

Federal Policy for Emergency Management

Directs federal organizations to:

  • conduct mandate-specific, all-hazards risk assessments, including those affected by critical infrastructure;
  • use common tools and best practices to conduct risk assessments;
  • develop programs, arrangements or measures for mitigating risks associated with their respective departments; and
  • apply and implement changes in collaboration with stakeholders based on lessons learned and best practices derived from training, exercises and real life response and recovery experiences.

The Government of Canada also has various strategies, measures, and programs that support the Emergency Management Act and the Federal Policy for Emergency Management. Click on the two examples below for more information:

Individual federal departments and agencies may also have their own mitigation strategies to address vulnerabilities within their own mandate.
