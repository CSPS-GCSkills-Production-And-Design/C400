Here is a quick recap of what you need to know about the steps involved in a risk assessment.

Step 1. Identify the risks

The Government of Canada uses an all-hazards risk approach to conduct risk assessments, which does not necessarily mean that all hazards need to be assessed, but rather that all hazards should be considered.

Step 2. Analyze the risks

Each hazard is then assessed as to its likelihood of occurrence and the expected impact. The degree of impact is often determined by the vulnerability of the region, community, facility or service.

Step 3. Evaluate the risks

Evaluating and prioritizing risks can separate the minor, acceptable ones from those that are unacceptable.

Step 4. Identify the risk prevention/mitigation options
  • avoid the risks
  • remove the source of the risks
  • change the nature or magnitude of the likelihood
  • change the consequences or impact
  • share the risk with another party
  • retain or ignore the risk
