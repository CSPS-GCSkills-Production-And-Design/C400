As our risk environment continues to evolve with increasing frequency and severity of hazards and threats to Canadians, this component of the emergency management process is becoming ever more important.

In this module, we have seen various examples of prevention/mitigation efforts from all levels of government. The Red River Floodway and the use of rain barrels are two examples of how serious flooding can be avoided.

Strategies should be collaborative and comprehensive. They should be based on all-hazards risk assessments and combined with targeted prevention/mitigation initiatives. When this occurs, the benefits can be counted in the saving of lives, property and livelihoods from the destructive impact of disasters.
