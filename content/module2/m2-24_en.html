Canada's Measures and Strategies

Disaster Mitigation and Adaptation Fund

In 2018, the Government of Canada launched the Disaster Mitigation and Adaptation Fund (DMAF), a 10-year national program to invest $2 billion in large-scale infrastructure projects that help communities better manage the risks of disasters triggered by natural hazards.

National Strategy for Critical Infrastructure

During an emergency, it is essential that Canada's critical infrastructure be protected.

 The goal of the National Strategy for Critical Infrastructure (2009) is to build a safer, more secure and more resilient Canada by enhancing the resiliency of critical infrastructure against current and emerging hazards. The Strategy supports stronger collaboration among FPT governments and the 10 critical infrastructure sectors, with a view to enhance the resiliency of critical infrastructure in Canada by:

  • building partnerships
  • implementing an all-hazards risk management approach
  • advancing the timely sharing and protection of information among partners

Critical Infrastructure

Critical infrastructure is the processes, systems, facilities, technologies, networks, assets and services essential to the health, safety, security or economic well-being of Canadians and the effective functioning of government.

Critical infrastructure can be stand-alone or interconnected and interdependent within and across provinces, territories and national borders.

10 critical infrastructure sectors

  1. Energy and utilities
  2. Finance
  3. Food
  4. Transportation
  5. Government
  6. Information and communication technology
  7. Health
  8. Water
  9. Safety
  10. Manufacturing

Did You Know?

Past events illustrate the importance of protecting critical infrastructure from all types of hazards.

  • 1996 Saguenay flood
  • 1997 and 2009 Red River floods
  • 1998 ice storm
  • 2003 power blackout
  • 2003 Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome (SARS)
