Hazard

A potentially damaging physical event, phenomenon or human activity that may cause the loss of life or injury, property damage, social and economic disruption or environmental degradation.

Threat

The presence of a hazard and an exposure pathway; threats may be natural or human-induced, either accidental or intentional.

Risk

The combination of the likelihood and the impact a specified hazard may have; refers to the vulnerability, proximity or exposure to hazards, which affects the likelihood of adverse impact.

Probability

The quantitative measurement of the possibilities that the foreseen outcome of an event or incident will occur.

Likelihood

The possibility of an event or incident happening,  measured in qualitative values (ex.: low, medium, or high). The resulting assessment is subjective.

Impact/Consequence

The degree to which the hazard would affect person, property, environment, social and economic activity.

Vulnerability

The conditions determined by physical, social, economic and environmental factors or processes, which increase the susceptibility of a community to the impact of hazards. It is a measure of how well-prepared and equipped a community is to minimize the impact of or cope with hazards.

Residual Risk

The risk remaining after countermeasures have been identified, selected, and implemented for the hazard/threat.
