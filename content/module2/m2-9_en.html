Hazard

A potentially damaging physical event, phenomenon or human activity that may cause the loss of life or injury, property damage, social economic disruption or environmental degradation.

Threat

The presence of a hazard and an exposure pathway.  A threat may be natural or human-induced, either accidental or intentional.

Risk

The combination of the likelihood and the impact a specified hazard may have; refers to the vulnerability, proximity or exposure to hazards, which affects the likelihood of adverse impact.

Probability

The quantitative measurement of the possibilities that the foreseen outcomes of an event or incident will occur based on parametersr of values. 

Likelihood

The possibility of an event or incident happening, measured in qualitative values (ex.: low, medium, or high).  The resulting assessment is subjective.

Impact/Consequence

The degree to which the hazard would affect a person, property, environment or social and economic activity.

Vulnerability

A condition or set of conditions determined by physical, social, economic and environmental factors or processes that increases the susceptibility of a community to the impact of hazards. Vulnerability is a measure of how well prepared and well-equipped a community is to minimize the impact of or cope with hazards.

Residual Risk

Risk that remains after implementing risk mitigation measures. (Government of Canada Emergency Management Vocabulary)

Did You Know?

The terms hazard and threat are often used interchangeably; however, threat often connotes malicious intent.
