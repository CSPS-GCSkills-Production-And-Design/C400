Vulnerability assessments look at inadequacies, weaknesses or gaps in the design, implementation or operation of an asset, system, network, or geographic area that could enable a threat or hazard to cause injury or disruption.

What conditions may be considered vulnerabilities?

  • personnel issues (e.g. high turnover, inadequately trained individuals)
  • insufficient secondary or support processes
  • existing EM plans that are immature or not tested

Did You Know?

Vulnerability assessments also consider the resilience of a community or system to potential vulnerabilities.

Resilience is defined in the Emergency Management Framework (2017) as “the capacity of a system, community or society to adapt to disturbances resulting from hazards by preserving, recuperating or changing to reach and maintain an acceptable level of functioning”.

Resilience minimizes vulnerability or susceptibility to damage from hazards. It creates or strengthens social and physical capacity in the human and built environment to cope with, adapt to, respond to, and recover and learn from emergency events.

All risk reduction should result in increased resiliency.
