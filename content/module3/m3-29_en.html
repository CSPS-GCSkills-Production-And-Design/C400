Recap – Lesson 3: Activities and Programs that Support Emergency Preparedness in Canada

Here are the key concepts you need to remember about preparedness activities and programs in Canada.

Training

Department-specific level

Each organization is responsible for developing EM plan within their areas of accountability and to conduct exercises and training as they relate to those plans. Key federal EM departments also offer mandate-specific EM training programs, courses and products.

Provincial/territorial government level

Each Canadian province and territory offers basic EM training. Some provinces and territories also offer specialty workshops and advanced-level training.

Local level

First responders and various municipal employees receive ongoing training to increase and maintain their EM skills. Formal EM learning is also becoming more accessible through various universities and colleges.

Exercising the Plan

National Exercise Program: Continuous improvement of emergency management in Canada through the coordination of comprehensive whole-of-government all-hazards exercises which help strengthen the capability across all regions to respond to incidents of all types.

Federal Public Awareness Programs

Emergency Preparedness Week

An annual national week-long event to increase awareness of individual preparedness. All Emergency Preparedness Week activities encourage Canadians to take actions that can reduce risks and lessen consequences of an emergency by being better prepared.

Getprepared.ca

This Public Safety website helps all Canadians prepare for emergencies by offering emergency preparedness guides that shows Canadians how to draw up an emergency plan and prepare a kit.

Public Health Agency Canada’s website www.phac-aspc.gc.ca provides health protection information to Canadians.

Environment and Climate Change Canada http://weather.gc.ca/ provides Public Weather Warnings for Canada.
