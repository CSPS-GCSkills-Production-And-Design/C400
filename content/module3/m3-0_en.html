Introduction

Welcome to this next module, Preparedness.

Canada and its people are facing an evolving risk environment due to the increasing severity and occurrence of natural hazards as well as human-induced threats including terrorism, globalized disease outbreaks, critical infrastructure interdependencies and cyber attacks.

Emergencies can quickly escalate in scope and severity, cross jurisdictional lines, take on international dimensions and result in significant human and economic losses.

The Government of Canada must prepare for such emergencies by strengthening its resiliency and by promoting an integrated and comprehensive approach against future potential hazards and threats.
