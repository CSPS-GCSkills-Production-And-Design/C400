Emergency Preparedness Week

Emergency Preparedness Week is an annual national event held during the first full week of May. It involves all levels of government and encourages Canadians to take actions to be better prepared during emergencies.

The main objective is to increase awareness of individual preparedness. All Emergency Preparedness Week activities reinforce the idea we can reduce risks and lessen consequences of an emergency by being better prepared.

Getprepared.ca

Public Safety Canada’s GetPrepared.ca site was developed to provide Canadians with practical information on preparing for emergencies.

http://www.getprepared.gc.ca

On the website, people can:

  • Find ways to prepare themselves and their families for an emergency.
  • Download the Government of Canada’s emergency preparedness guide for families and for people with disabilities/special needs.
  • Complete the online emergency plan.
  • Sign up to receive monthly “safety tip” emails from Public Safety Canada.
  • Share a story about an emergency experienced and lessons learned.
  • Watch informative videos online about making an emergency kit and creating a family emergency plan.
