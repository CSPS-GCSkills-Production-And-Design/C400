Lead Security Agencies

The Policy on Government Security identifies lead security agencies and organisations that have a leadership and support role and that contribute to the achievement of government security policy objectives. Look to see if your department is one of the lead agencies and consider its roles and responsibilities.

Click on each department for more information.

Treasury Board Secretariat

Treasury Board Secretariat (TBS) is responsible for the following:

  • Establishing and overseeing a whole-of-government approach to Security management as a key component of all management activities by ensuring the conduct of periodic reviews of the effectiveness of security support services, to provide assurance that they continue to meet the needs of the government as a whole;
  • Providing policy leadership, advice and guidance for all matters related to government Security; and
  • Providing strategic policy oversight and coordination for the management of security events that may affect the government as a whole.

Privy Council Office

Privy Council Office (PCO) is responsible for the following:

  • Providing leadership, technical advice and guidance for matters related to business continuity management;
  • Providing operational leadership for the coordination, information sharing and situational awareness relating to security events involving multiple Federal Departments or Agencies that may have government-wide, intergovernmental, critical infrastructure or national impacts;
  • Providing leadership in establishing the necessary arrangements for the continuity of constitutional government in the event of an emergency; and
  • Leading coordination and strategic policy-making on national security and national cyber security matters.

Public Safety Canada

Public Safety Canada (PS) is responsible for the following:

  • Providing leadership, technical advice and guidance for matters related to business continuity management;
  • Providing operational leadership for the coordination, information sharing and situational awareness relating to security events involving multiple Federal Departments or Agencies that may have government-wide, intergovernmental, critical infrastructure or national impacts;
  • Providing leadership in establishing the necessary arrangements for the continuity of constitutional government in the event of an emergency; and
  • Leading coordination and strategic policy-making on national security and national cyber security matters.

Communications Security Establishment

Communications Security Establishment (CSE) is responsible for the following:

  • Serving as the lead technical authority for information technology (IT) security, including the provision of leadership, advice, services and guidance for technical matters related to IT security;
  • Helping to ensure the protection of electronic information and of information infrastructures of importance to the Government of Canada;
  • Fulfilling the following government-wide functions:
    • Identifying emerging cyber threats;
    • Defending government networks and systems; and
    • Protecting against, and mitigating potential impacts of, cyber security events;
  • Leading the development of trusted sources of supply for government and critical infrastructure, and mitigating the risk of untrusted equipment;
  • Serving as the national authority for communications security (COMSEC), including the procurement, distribution, control and use of cryptographic devices and encryption keying material for national security systems; and
  • Serving as Canada’s national authority for signals intelligence (SIGINT).

Public Services and Procurement Canada

Public Services and Procurement Canada (PSPC) is responsible for the following:

  • Providing leadership, advice and guidance for matters related to contract security;
  • Supporting and fulfilling government-wide functions for issuing personal record identifiers (PRI) to departments and agencies and individual agency numbers (IAN) to agencies outside the federal public service, and maintaining the PRI and IAN systems;
  • Providing emergency procurement and emergency accommodation, and providing security services to help ensure the protection of sensitive information entrusted to Canadian and foreign industry;
  • Providing internal enterprise services for contract security, base building security for general-purpose office facilities under its custodial responsibility, and IT security in support of providing and managing certain government-wide applications; and
  • Serving as the government’s national authority for industrial security, and in this capacity, serving as Canada’s Designated Security Authority for NATO.

Canadian Security Intelligence Service

The Canadian Security Intelligence Service (CSIS) is responsible for the following:

  • Providing government-wide services in security screening;
  • Fulfilling government-wide functions by investigating and analysing threats to the security of Canada and by providing related reporting and advice to the Government of Canada; and
  • Maintaining a central registry for the retention of forms that designate persons permanently bound to secrecy under the Security of Information Act.

Royal Canadian Mounted Police

Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP) is responsible for the following:

  • Providing leadership, advice and guidance for matters related to physical security;
  • Fulfilling government-wide functions related to criminal threat intelligence and criminal investigations; and
  • Providing government-wide services related to security screening, technical surveillance countermeasures, and safeguarding of designated persons.

Shared Services Canada

Shared Services Canada (SSC) is responsible for the following:

  • Planning, designing, building, operating and maintaining effective, efficient and responsive enterprise IT security infrastructure services to secure Government of Canada data and systems under its responsibility.

Global Affairs Canada

Global Affairs Canada is responsible for the following:

  • Providing leadership, advice and guidance regarding security at missions abroad, and conducting Canada’s international relations on matters related to government security;
  • Fulfilling government-wide functions related to security developments abroad, and providing services to departments abroad to ensure security at missions; and
  • Serving as Canada’s National Security Authority for NATO.

Department of National Defence

Department of National Defence (DND) is responsible for the following:

  • Fulfilling government-wide functions for scientific and technological security research, defence intelligence, and investigation of security threats to military systems;
  • Providing support to departments in relation to the protection of Government of Canada officials outside Canada, cyber security, and the provision of other security-related services;
  • Providing support to Public Safety Canada in relation to the continuity of constitutional government and domestic counterterrorism;
  • Serving as Canada’s National Distribution Authority for NATO (North Atlantic Treaty Organization); and
  • Serving as Canada’s national authority for Talent-Keyhole (TK) information.
