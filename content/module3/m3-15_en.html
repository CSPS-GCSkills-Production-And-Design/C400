Recap – Lesson 2: Emergency Management Planning

Here is a quick recap of the key elements of the Government of Canada's approach to emergency preparedness planning.

Click on each heading for a recap of lesson two.

Federal Management Plan

Federal Government’s all-hazards response plan which outlines the processes and mechanisms to facilitate an integrated Government of Canada response to an emergency; includes each federal organization’s responsibilities for emergency support functions.

Emergency Management Plans (EMP)

Establish a federal government organization’s objectives, approach and structure for protecting Canadians and Canada from threats and hazards in its areas of responsibility.

Operational Plans

Offers threat and hazard specific plans that support each organization’s Emergency Management Plan.

Business Continuity Plans (BCP)

Provide for the continued availability of critical services delivered by federal organizations, and for the recovery of their operations in the event of an emergency or disruption.

Canada's Continuity of Constitutional Government Emergency Response and Recovery Plan (CERRP)

Outlines a process for responding to a threat or catastrophic event that would require the re-situation of the organizations of constitutional government to an alternate site outside of the National Capital Region.

The plan aims to help ensure that, during a catastrophic emergency, the constitutional authority and decision-making of the executive, legislative and judicial branches of the government are maintained.
