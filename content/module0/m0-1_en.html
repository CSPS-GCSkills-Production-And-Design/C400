Course Objectives

After completing this course on federal basic emergency management, you will be able to:

  • Identify the driving force that gives authority and responsibility to the Government of Canada for emergency management, including the legislation, policies, plans and frameworks.
  • Describe what “emergency management” means, including what constitutes an emergency, the changing context for emergency management and potential impacts on federal government business.
  • Describe the Government of Canada’s roles for each component of emergency management:
    • Prevention/Mitigation
    • Preparedness
    • Response
    • Recovery
  • Explain how the federal government coordinates its efforts with provincial/territorial and local governments, as well as private sectors and international partners, to provide an effective collaborative approach to emergency management in Canada.
